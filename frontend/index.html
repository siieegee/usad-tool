How to Understand the Results

1

Decision

The model labels a review as Normal or Suspicious based on the overall wording pattern compared to typical reviews.

2

Confidence

A higher percentage means the model is more certain. Low confidence usually means the review is near the decision boundary.

3

Margin

Margin is the difference between the review's distance and the threshold. Larger positive margins indicate stronger anomalies.

4

Key Warning Signs

When available, short notes explain the most important signals driving a "Suspicious" result (e.g., high repetition or extreme tone).

5

Feature Criteria

Each feature shows Value, a Criteria range, and a Status badge. "Pass" means typical; "Warning" highlights unusual values.

Feature Glossary

Length of review

How many words are in the review. Very short reviews can look suspicious.

Word variety

How varied the words are. More unique words usually mean a more natural review.

Average word length

Average number of letters per word. Extremely short or long words can be unusual.

Overall tone

Overall positivity or negativity. Extremely high or low values can be unnatural.

Opinion level

How opinion-based the review is. Very subjective without details can be suspicious.

Language complexity

How complex or varied the wording is. Low complexity may indicate template-like text.

Word repetition

How often words are repeated. High repetition is common in fake reviews.

Exclamation marks

How many exclamation marks are used. Too many can be a red flag.

Question marks

How many question marks are used. Unusual punctuation patterns can be suspicious.

Capital letter usage

How much of the text is CAPITALIZED. Excessive caps can look promotional.

Punctuation density

How dense the punctuation is. Unusual patterns can be a signal.

Tip: Hover the ? icons in the Results to see simple explanations of each feature.
